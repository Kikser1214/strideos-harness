# Support Fueling

Provide optional, practical fueling support that fits the athlete's training and preferences. Keep uncertainty visible and never turn ordinary wellness guidance into diagnosis, treatment, or a rigid diet plan.

Read [fueling-boundaries.md](references/fueling-boundaries.md) before using meal images, numeric targets, supplements, allergies, or prescribed diets.

## Confirm the mode

Ask whether the athlete wants nutrition support and choose one mode:

- `off`: no nutrition coaching;
- `loose`: simple meal and timing suggestions without tracking;
- `guided`: portions, patterns, and session-specific guidance;
- `detailed`: optional numeric estimates when appropriate and explicitly wanted;
- `number_free`: no calorie, macro, or weight targets.

Number-free preference, under-18 status, a relevant tracking concern, `do_not_use` weight context, or clinician-prescribed constraints override detailed tracking.

## Gather only useful context

Collect dietary pattern, allergies and intolerances, prescribed restrictions, budget, cooking and storage access, routine meals, hydration, training time, protein powder, creatine, caffeine, other supplements, relevant medications, and preferences. Do not assume any supplement is needed.

## Give practical guidance

- Tie advice to the actual session, timing, climate, duration, and tolerance.
- Prefer ordinary foods and flexible options the athlete can realistically obtain.
- Explain pre-run, during-run, and recovery purpose in plain language.
- Treat race fueling as something to rehearse in training, not introduce on race day.
- Distinguish general hydration habits from medical electrolyte or fluid advice.
- For supplements, explain likely relevance, evidence limits, dose uncertainty, interaction risk, and when a qualified clinician or dietitian is appropriate.

## Handle meal or fridge images

1. Confirm the athlete selected the image and permits the configured processing route.
2. Describe visible foods and uncertainty. Ask about ingredients, sauces, preparation, cooking fats, portions, and anything hidden.
3. Never claim an image is safe for an allergy or cross-contact risk.
4. Offer a practical meal idea or estimate consistent with the selected mode.
5. Keep the estimate separate from corrections.
6. Log it only after the athlete confirms the exact normalized record through its linked approval.
7. Preserve deletion for the normalized record and do not retain the raw image in the bundled local state.

## Close safely

State what is an estimate and what the athlete confirmed. If the request involves an eating disorder concern, a medically prescribed diet, significant symptoms, medication interaction, pregnancy, or aggressive weight manipulation, stop numeric coaching and recommend qualified care.
